What should I know about VAT fraud and carousel fraud?
VAT carousel fraud (MTIC fraud) involves multiple fake businesses trading goods to manipulate VAT rebates. Carousel fraud costs EU states billions annually. Prevention: verify all suppliers' VAT legitimacy, check business registration history, monitor for unusual trading patterns. What is an EU VAT number?
An EU VAT number (VAT ID) is a country-specific tax identifier for businesses registered for VAT in EU member states. It follows the format: 2-letter country code + up to 12 digits. Example: DE123456789012 (Germany). Used for intra-EU business transactions and compliance.

What are the VAT compliance requirements in Germany?
German VAT compliance requires: (1) registering for Umsatzsteuer-ID if turnover exceeds €22,000, (2) filing monthly/quarterly VAT returns, (3) maintaining invoice records, (4) charging correct VAT rates (19% standard, 7% reduced), (5) participating in EU VAT reporting if operating across borders.

What is the VAT registration threshold in Germany?
In Germany, businesses must register for VAT (Umsatzsteuer) if their annual turnover exceeds €22,000. If turnover is below this threshold, VAT registration is optional. New businesses must register immediately if they exceed this limit within their first year.

What are red flags for fraudulent businesses?
Red flags include: invalid or mismatched VAT numbers, newly registered companies with high-value offers, spelling errors in business names or emails, pressure for immediate payment, requests to wire funds outside EU, vague business descriptions, and newly created websites. What information must be on a VAT invoice?
Valid VAT invoices in Germany must include: invoice number & date, supplier & customer VAT numbers, itemized description, net amount, VAT amount & rate, total amount, payment terms, and business names/addresses. Digital invoices are acceptable if they meet security standards.

What is the reverse charge mechanism for VAT?
The reverse charge (Reverse Charge) shifts VAT liability from seller to buyer in B2B transactions, typically for intra-EU or international supplies. The buyer accounts for VAT instead of the seller. Common in construction, services, and digital supplies. Requires both parties to have valid VAT numbers.

What is Intrastat reporting?
Intrastat is the system for collecting trade statistics on goods movements between EU member states. Businesses trading goods within the EU must report monthly/quarterly. The report includes commodity codes, values, and trade partners' VAT numbers. Threshold: €400,000 (monthly) or €1 million (quarterly).

What is the One-Stop Shop (OSS) VAT scheme?
The OSS (One-Stop Shop) allows non-EU and EU sellers to file a single VAT return for EU sales instead of registering in each country. Available for distance sales, digital services, and B2C supplies under €35,000 (EU sellers). Simplifies compliance for cross-border e-commerce and reduces administrative burden.
